Mahatma Gandhi


"A eye for an eye makes the whole world blind"

Accomplishments

  • Rabindranath Tagore, a great Indian polymath,
    accorded the title of “Mahatma” (meaning “high-souled"
    or “venerable" in Sanskrit), to Mohandas Karamchand Gandhi.
  • ‘Time’ magazine named Gandhi the Man of the Year in 1930.
  • Gandhi was nominated for the Nobel Peace Prize
    five times between 1937 and 1948 though he was never awarded the prize.
    The Nobel Committee publicly declared its regret for the omission decades later.
  • Gandhi was a prolific writer and penned several books including the autobiographies
    ‘The Story of My Experiments with Truth’, ‘Satyagraha in South Africa’,
    and ‘Hind Swaraj or Indian Home Rule’.
